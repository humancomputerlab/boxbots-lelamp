## Overview

LeLamp consists of three main sections: the **Head**, the **Base**, and the **Body**, each containing essential functional components.

### LeLamp Head

- **LED Matrix** ‚Äî WS2812B programmable LEDs for expression

### LeLamp Base

- **Raspberry Pi with ReSpeaker Hat** ‚Äî Controls servos, audio, and LEDs
- **Motor Driver** ‚Äî Drives the servo motors

### LeLamp Body

- **Motors** - Actuators of the Lamp
- **Speaker** ‚Äî 4 Œ© / 3 W audio output

There will be a power, data and ground cable routed all the way to the head for the LEDs, along with a usb cable for the camera.

## Soldering and Assembly

### 1. Assembling the LeLamp Base

![](./assets/images/2_lamp_base.jpeg)

1. **Place inserts on electronics mounts**
   - Put down M2.5 heatset inserts on the **tall** electronics mounts
   - Put down M3 heatset inserts where the servos are mounted (the **short** mounts in the middle)
1. **Mount the electronics:**
   - Secure the **motor driver board** and **Raspberry Pi** inside the base as shown above with M2.5 screws (8mm).
   - Fit the **12V-5V stepdown board** snugly underneath the motor driver board (no mounts for this one, sorry)
   - The last **tall** mounts can be used for the speaker
   - The **short** mounts is for the base core (which contains two motors)
1. **Connect the boards:**

   - Use a **USB Type-C cable** to connect the motor driver to the Raspberry Pi (see image above)
   - Connect the stepdown board with a **USB Type-C cable** to the Raspberry Pi (see image above)
   - Connect the stepdown board from the screw terminal (next to the barrel connector) to the motor driver board's green screw terminal

## Motor Assembly

![](./assets/images/4_sts3215.jpg)

Each STS3215 servo motor comes with two motor horns ‚Äî one with gears and one without. Before mounting the servos onto the lamp structure, first attach the geared horn to the motor‚Äôs output side (the side with the copper gear) and secure it firmly with screws. On the opposite side, place the non-geared horn; this one does not need to be screwed in, as it serves only for axis alignment.

Because the motor body is asymmetrical, the orientation of the motor horns is critical to ensure proper movement and reliability. Refer to the reference images below to confirm correct positioning.

Refer to the schematics or the lamp base image above for proper wiring orientation and connection order.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è _Reminder:_ If your motor wire isn't long enough, you have to extend them through another wire by [splicing the wires](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r7GlDBge_WU).

### 3. Assembling the LeLamp Head

Connect the LED matrix in the head to the Raspberry Pi hat in the base. The image below shows the general wiring layout:

![](./assets/images/led_assembly.jpeg)

1. **Connect to the Pi hat:** Use a **female connector** that plugs into the Raspberry Pi hat's LED output pins.

2. **Route the wire:** Run the wire from the base all the way up to the head. You can solder good quality wire in between to extend the connection as needed. Ensure the wire is at least **50 cm** long to reach from the head to the base.

3. **Connect to the LED matrix:** The head comes with preset LED connectors. Simply plug your wire into these connectors as in the image above.

4. **Follow color conventions:**

   - **Black** ‚Äî GND
   - **Red** ‚Äî 5V
   - **Yellow** ‚Äî Data

5. If you're unfamiliar with WS2812B LED wiring, refer to this [tutorial](https://lastminuteengineers.com/ws2812b-arduino-tutorial/).

6. The image above also indicates the **orientation and placement** of the LED matrix inside the head.
7. Finally, push the lamp shade cover (the one that goes in front of the LED) into the lamp shade.

## Power

You'll need 2 power supplies to power LeLamp:

1. **A 5V 2A DC Power Supply With DC Plug**: This is used to provide power to the servos.
2. **A 5V 2A Type C Charger**: This is used to provide power to the Rasperry Pi.

You can reduce the amount of power supply to 1 by providing the 5V from the motor driver to the pi. However, this is not recommended and has high risk of bricking your Pi.
